What Is Cryptocurrency? Is Crypto Halal?

You’ve probably heard the words ‘cryptocurrency’, ‘blockchain’ and ‘NFT’ in the news or from your Gen Z cousin. But what are they exactly? How can you get started investing in crypto safely? And as a Muslim, someone who holds an Abrahamic faith, or just upholds ethics to a high standard, you must be wondering — is crypto halal? Can it be interest-free? Well, let’s find out!

Blockchain, Cryptocurrency and NFT Explained

To understand cryptocurrency, we must first look at the distributed ledger technology (DLT) upon which crypto is built i.e. blockchain. Simply put, a blockchain is a database of transactions. Think of it like an Excel database, except that information entered into each cell or block cannot be modified or deleted. The blocks of information are added in a linear fashion, creating a chain of blocks — hence blockchain!

Where is the blockchain database stored?

Information on the blockchain is stored on a network of computers called nodes. At least more than half of the nodes need to reach a consensus for new information to be added. There are several types of nodes but the two most common ones that validate transactions are miner nodes and staking nodes.

  • Miner nodes, which are part of the Proof-of-Work (PoW) algorithm, requires significant computer power and energy consumption to successfully add a block to the chain.
  • Staking nodes, which are part of the Proof-of-Stake (PoS) algorithm, validate transactions by staking money upon selection based on pre-defined rules such as coinage or time spent on the network.

What is cryptocurrency?

The information contained in a block consists of different forms of data. One of it could be a digital asset like cryptocurrency. It’s a form of currency that exists digitally and uses cryptography to secure transactions. By nature, cryptocurrencies do not have a regulating authority and thus it is considered a decentralized form of currency.

Other attributes of cryptocurrency include:

  • Pseudonymous: you don’t have to provide your personal information to own and use cryptocurrency; anyone can use it.
  • Peer-to-peer: cryptocurrencies are transferred from one person to another without having to go through an intermediary.
  • Trustless: since there is no third-party control, users are in complete control of their cryptocurrency.
  • Borderless: cryptocurrencies can be sent to anyone anywhere in the world much easier than fiat currencies.
  • Encrypted: secured by cryptography, it’s nearly impossible to hack the information contained in a cryptocurrency.

Cryptocurrencies can be transferred using a public key and accessed with a private key.

We can compare it to sending an email to another person. If you want someone to send you an email (crypto), you would need to give them your email address (public key). If you want to read said email, you would have to enter your password (private key).

What about NFTs?

Non-fungible tokens (NFTs) are also digital assets. It differs from cryptocurrency in the sense that NFTs are one-of-a-kind, unique digital identifiers that cannot be copied, substituted, or subdivided. Think of cryptocurrency as cash and NFTs as a Mona Lisa painting or Pokemon cards.

Are Digital Assets Halal?

According to MRHB DeFi’s Shariah Governance Board, blockchain and smart contracts as froms of technology are permissible because there is nothing inherently impermissible in them.

Cryptocurrencies are essentially used as money or a medium of exchange. The concept of money was never defined directly in the Quran and Sunnah and so anything other than gold and silver can be used as money.

However, a statement by Umar bin al-Khattab defied this notion:

“And Umar bin Al-Khattab said: I wanted to make dirhams out of camel’s skins. So, it was said to him: then there will be no camel, then he held back [from this idea]” — (Al-Biladhuri, 1984, p. 452)

As such, money can take any form as long as that form does not intrinsically consist of impermissible or haram elements such as pig skin.

In that same vein, NFTs can only be considered as Shariah-compliant if their underlying object, project and features are free from prohibited elements.

Why Use Crypto

If you resonate with the values of political freedom and autonomy, freedom of choice, confidentiality and security, then cryptocurrency can pave the way for such ideals.

Besides the attributes as mentioned earlier, cryptocurrency alsoenables commerce at a lower cost than is possible with traditional forms.

The Slight Downside of Crypto

While the potential of cryptocurrency in most part is promising and beneficial, you should still be aware of the current and potential issues it presents.

  1. False decentralization While cryptocurrency is a decentralized digital asset by nature, the transaction or governance of cryptocurrency may not be completely decentralized. A large enough group of people verifying blockchain transactions does form a quasi-centralized entity. However, since every decision is made by the community and not one single authority, it is not exactly centralized either.
  2. Inefficiency Theoretically, the transfer of cryptocurrencies should be quick as there is no middleman to go through. However, in practice, some cryptocurrency transfers take longer than the electronic transfer of fiat currency. This is especially so on protocols using PoW verification model, which consumes up a lot of energy leading to high network costs and high greenhouse gas emissions. As such, to ensure faster and lower cost transactions, MRHB DeFi leverages the BEP20 token standard on the Binance Smart Chain, which uses the Proof-of-Staked-Authority model which is very much less energy-intensive and provides much faster and lower-cost transactions.
  3. Scams & Hacks These are issues that have been plaguing crypto almost since the beginning. This is most common in centralized exchanges, protocols and projects with no real value. By focusing on decentralization, building a secure ecosystem, and implementing ethical considerations, MRHB DeFi aims to be a part of the solution.
  4. Volatility As cryptocurrency’s value is not yet stable, its price still fluctuates. However, to mitigate the volatility issue, some cryptocurrencies like USDT and USDC are pegged to fiat currency and others to gold. When cryptocurrency adoption becomes more common, we can expect the price to stabilize.

Who Controls Crypto?

You may be inclined to think that it is the computers that control crypto. Well, if you think about it, the codes governing cryptocurrency and their transactions are computed by humans in the first place.

Just like fiat, there are four key players in the crypto financial market:

  • Small fishes: any crypto investor who doesn’t usually make a huge impact on the crypto market.
  • Whales: individuals or groups of individuals that have the ability to shake the market.
  • Institutions: these are the creators of cryptocurrency that can directly add value to or disrupt the quality of the crypto market.
  • Governments: regulations from the government can directly impact the crypto market as they may impose restrictions to its citizens from participating in the market.
